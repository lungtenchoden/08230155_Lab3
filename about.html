About Me

I’m in my third year of studying Education in IT, and I was born on June 16, 2004. From an early age, I've been curious about how people learn and how technology can support that process. My journey has been shaped by both personal dreams and the quiet hopes of my parents. Teaching has always felt right to me, not just as a profession, but as something I genuinely care about. Working with children has shown me how unpredictable and thoughtful their questions can be. They challenge me in ways I didn’t expect and remind me that being a teacher means being a learner too.

Switching from Arts in high school to ICT in college was a big shift. It wasn’t easy, but it taught me how to adapt, stay curious, and keep going even when things felt uncertain. I want to create classrooms that feel welcoming and alive with technology, creativity, and kindness at the center. A space where students feel safe to ask, explore, and grow.

I’m inspired by quiet moments, meaningful places, and stories that stay with me. They shape how I see the world and how I hope to guide others through it. Teaching, to me, is more than a profession; it’s a journey of growth, curiosity, and connection.
