Projects

Physics Project: Working with Bipolar Junction Transistors

As part of a group activity in our Physics class, we worked on an experiment to understand how a bipolar junction transistor (BJT) functions, specifically using the BC547. We set up a biasing circuit on a breadboard and tested how the transistor responded to different voltages, observing its behavior in modes like active and saturation. Through this hands-on process, we learned how transistors manage current flow and why proper biasing is key. It was a great opportunity to explore electronics practically and deepen our understanding of how these tiny components play a big role in everyday devices.

DDD Project: Mini Project on Database Design

As a team, we worked on building a Sports Event Management System for the Bhutan University Sports Federation (BUSF), designed to make organizing inter-college sports events smoother and more efficient. We focused on features like event registration, scheduling, team and participant management, and result tracking. Throughout the project, we learned how to turn real-world challenges into practical solutions using technology. It was a great experience that helped us grow our skills in system design, collaboration, and applying digital tools to improve coordination across multiple colleges.

Future Project: Gym Management System

We are planning to develop a digital platform for the Samtse College of Education (SCE) gym that will allow students and staff to register online, track their attendance, and manage membership payments. The system will also include features for monitoring equipment usage, scheduling workout sessions, and providing important gym-related updates. By digitizing these services, the platform aims to reduce manual paperwork, improve record accuracy, and save time for both users and gym staff. Ultimately, the Gym Management System will ensure a more efficient, transparent, and user-friendly experience for the entire SCE community.
